## Abstract 

Pan-sharpening technology refers to generating a high-resolution (HR) multi-spectral (MS) image with broad ap-plications by fusing a low-resolution (LR) MS image and HR panchromatic (PAN) image. While deep learning approaches have shown impressive performance in pan-sharpening, they generally require extensive hardware with high memory and computational power, limiting their de-ployment on resource-constrained satellites. In this study, we investigate the use of binary neural networks (BNNs) for pan-sharpening and observe that binarization leads to distinct information degradation across different frequency components of an image. Building on this insight, we pro-pose a novel binary pan-sharpening network, termed BN-NPan, structured around the Prior-Integrated Binary Fre-quency (PIBF) module that features three key ingredients: Binary Wavelet Transform Convolution, Latent Diffusion Prior Compensation, and Channel-wise Distribution Cal-ibration. Specifically, the first decomposes input features into distinct frequency components using Wavelet Trans-form, then applies a “divide-and-conquer” strategy to opti-mize binary feature learning for each component, informed by the corresponding full-precision residual statistics. The second integrates a latent diffusion prior to compensate for compromised information during binarization, while the third performs channel-wise calibration to further refine feature representation. Our BNNPan, developed with the proposed techniques, achieves promising pan-sharpening performance on multiple remote sensing datasets, surpass-ing state-of-the-art binarization algorithms.

Our motivation. Although state-of-the-art DL-based ap-proaches deliver impressive pan-sharpening performance, they typically require substantial computational resources, such as high-end GPUs, rendering them impractical for re-mote sensing satellites with limited processing and stor-age capacities. To address this challenge, we investi-gate network binarization as a potential solution for pan-sharpening, aiming to reduce computational demands while preserving performance levels for deployment feasibility. We first analyze the variations in real-valued activations after the binarization process. As shown in Fig. 1, the binarized features encounter significant information loss compared to the full-precision features. Given the pixel-level precision required for pan-sharpening, these losses normally result in substantial performance degradation. Herein, we address this challenging issue from a fundamen-tal image processing perspective. Specifically, we investi-gate the effects of binarization on different frequency com-ponents, recognizing that each component provides unique content information. As illustrated in Fig. 1, our analysis re-veals that: 1) different frequency components respond dif-ferently to binarization; and 2) applying binarization at the frequency level preserves a greater amount of feature infor-mation. This insight motivates the adoption of frequency-wise binary feature learning for pan-sharpening. In addition, to address the inherent distributional dispar-ity between binarized and full-precision features, we de-velop a full-precision statistics normalized binary convolu-tion (BConv) unit. As shown in Fig. 2, unlike the conven-tional BConv unit, our design introduces a lightweight nor-malization step that leverages full-precision residual statis-tics to redistribute the binary output, effectively narrowing the value gap with minimal computational overhead. The t-SNE visualization in Fig. 2 further demonstrates that our normalization approach tightly clusters the binary activa-tions with their full-precision counterparts, significantly en-hancing representational capability. Building on this, we propose an effective Binary Wavelet Transform Convolu-tion (BWTC) block that allows different frequency com-ponents to attain optimal feature learning, tailored to their specific characteristics and full-precision statistics. Fol-lowing BWTC, we further integrate a latent diffusion prior to compensate for the information lost during binarization. Lastly, channel-wise attention is introduced to refine the en-riched feature representations. With these core innovations, BNNPan achieves competitive performance relative to full-precision and binary baselines across multiple datasets. Our contributions are summarized as follows: • We propose BNNPan, a streamlined and efficient binary network tailored for remote sensing multi-spectral image fusion, delivering promising results with lower computa-tional requirements. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first attempt to introduce a binary neural network for pan-sharpening learning. • We devise a novel binary convolution unit by introducing a computation-negligible normalization subsequent to its vanilla counterpart, which utilizes full-precision residual statistics to redistribute the output of binary convolution, effectively narrowing the value gap. • We introduce a latent diffusion module (LDM) to gener-ate comprehensive and compact image feature represen-tations, functioning as a degradation-free prior to com-pensating for the information lost during the binarization process through a three-stage training strategy. • Experiments on multiple remote sensing benchmark datasets show that the proposed BNNPan outperforms state-of-the-art binary neural networks. 

## 2. Related Work 

2.1. Deep Learning-based Pan-sharpening 

Over the past years, deep learning technology has made im-pressive strides across various low-level vision tasks [5, 8, 10, 27, 41]. These significant advantages have also per-meated pan-sharpening and have taken a leading role in this field. PNN [22] stands as a pioneering effort, utiliz-ing a straightforward three-layer CNN architecture that per-forms competitively with traditional algorithms. Following 2237 this, PanNet [36] first introduces the residual learning [11] in pan-sharpening, leading to a deeper network architec-ture. Afterward, an unprecedented surge of research inter-est in DL-based pan-sharpening methods arises, resulting in a flood of complicated networks designed to improve pan-sharpening performance [6, 13, 19, 20, 25, 43, 44]. Fusion-Net [6] proposes a robust CNN architecture by revisiting the principles of traditional MRA and CS algorithms. Guid-edNet [25] constructs a general multi-scale pan-sharpening network via a progressive learning strategy. Hou et al . de-velop the band-wise modeling paradigms [13, 20] grounded in a fundamental observation of the inherent discrepancies among various spectral bands in MS modality. Along-side CNN-based methods, other advanced DL approaches have also been proposed, such as transformer models [14, 23, 42], diffusion models [3, 34], to further improve pan-sharpening performance. Although these deep learning models have made significant strides in pan-sharpening compared to traditional algorithms, they necessitate pow-erful hardware with substantial memory and computational resources, limiting their applications over resource-limited satellites. In this work, we aim to probe the potential of binarized neural networks for pan-sharpening. 

2.2. Binarized Neural Network 

Binarization is the most extreme model quantization tech-nique, delivering remarkable compression by limiting net-work weights and activations to binary values of -1 and +1, which significantly decreases storage and computa-tional resources. Hubara et al . [15] pioneer the concept of model binarization, wherein network weights and acti-vations are directly binarized during the training process. Following this, Rastegari et al . [26] introduce scaling fac-tors for weights and activations in XNOR-Net, contribut-ing to a 58-fold speedup and a 32-fold reduction in mem-ory usage. Recently, there has been growing interest in the exploration of binary networks for low-level vision applica-tions [2, 4, 16, 17, 28, 32, 33, 38, 40]. Xin et al . [33] first de-velop a complete binary image super-resolution (SR) model that binarizes both network weights and activations, un-leashing the potential of binary SR network. Jiang et al . [16] present a novel binary training mechanism that omits the batch normalization layers. Xia et al . [32] propose a basic binary convolution unit (BBCU) for image restoration by analyzing the functions of each ingredient within it. In [17], the authors separately address the high-frequency and low-frequency components. Chen et al . [4] introduce a novel binarized diffusion model for image SR. Cai et al . [2] pi-oneer the application of binary network for hyperspectral image reconstruction. Indeed, remote sensing satellites, as quintessential resource-constrained devices, have a pressing demand for model quantization. This necessity stems from several critical and practical factors associated with satellite deployment, such as computational power, storage capacity, energy consumption, and others. Consequently, we seek to investigate the capabilities of binary networks for remote sensing multi-spectral image fusion. 

## 3. Methodology 

3.1. Overall Architecture 

As illustrated in Fig. 3, the overall framework of our pro-posed BNNPan consists of two parts: (1) a Latent Diffusion Module; (2) a backbone network built with Prior-Integrated Binary Frequency (PIBF) modules. Specifically, we first employ a Latent Diffusion Model to obtain a highly com-pact latent feature ˆz ∈ RC , which contains overall im-age information, serving as a prior to compensate for in-formation lost during binarization. Note that C denotes the number of channels of latent features. Then, we perform pan-sharpening learning using a network equipped with the PIBF module, aided by the prior feature ˆz. Mathematically, we can formulate the mapping function of the proposed BN-NPan as follows: 

ˆz = φ(zT , c ) = φ(zT , LE(Cat( P, MU ))) ,

SR = N (P, MU , ˆz) + MU , (1) where c denotes the condition which is produced by the latent encoder using PAN image P and MU image, up-sampled from MS image M, as input. zT is a noisy latent feature sampled from the pure Gaussian distribution. ˆz is the latent feature generated by the diffusion model with a function of φ(·). Cat( ·) and LE( ·) represent the mapping of channel-wise concatenation operation and the latent en-coder. N (·) is the function of the main pan-sharpening net-work, while SR is the resulting high-resolution MS image. 

3.2. Prior-Integrated Binary Frequency Module 

As shown in Fig. 3, our proposed prior-integrated bi-nary frequency (PIBF) module constitutes the main pan-sharpening network. Each PIBF module comprises three in-gredients including Binary Wavelet Transform Convolution (BWTC), Latent Diffusion Prior Compensation (LDPC), and Channel-wise Distribution Calibration (CDC).

Latent Diffusion Prior Compensation. The binary net-work inevitably faces a natural performance bottleneck due to the intrinsic information loss caused by the binarization process. To alleviate this, we integrate degradation-free prior to compensate for the compromised feature informa-tion during the binarization process. Specifically, we intro-duce a latent diffusion module to generate a compact prior feature ˆz which is integrated into the outcome of the BWTC, leading to more informative feature representations. As il-lustrated in Fig. 4, we first employ a linear transformation to the latent prior feature ˆz, followed by the channel-wise split to achieve two parts, which are then integrated into the output of the BWTC through a scaling factor zw ∈ RC and shift basis zb ∈ RC , respectively. Formally, we can formu-late this process as follows: 

zw, z b = Split(Linear(ˆ z)) ,

eY = Y ⊙ zw + zb, (8) where Linear( ·) and Split( ·) denote the mapping of the lin-ear transformation and the channel splitting operation. ⊙ is the element-wise multiplication. In practice, we implement this process in a three-phase manner, as depicted in Fig. 3. In the first phase, we design a latent encoder to compress texture-rich HRMS ( i.e ., ground truth) image GT into the latent space, resulting in the latent prior feature z ∈ RC , which is defined as: 

z = LE( GT ), (9) where LE( ·) is mapping of the latent encoder. As shown in Fig. 3, it is implemented with a very simple and com-putationally efficient structure consisting of only a convo-lutional projection layer, average pooling, and two linear layers coupled with LeakyReLU activation. Then, the com-pressed prior feature z is fed into the PIBF module to gener-ate SR image. Notably, we only use the image reconstruc-tion loss in this phase to optimize the latent encoder and main network as: 

L1 = ∥GT − SR∥ 1 . (10) After learning the latent prior representation from GT images, we aim to train a latent diffusion model to gener-ate this prior in the second phase. ## 5. Conclusion 

In this paper, we pioneer the exploration of the binary neu-ral network for pan-sharpening and propose a novel so-lution, i.e ., BNNPan, built upon the fundamental prior-integrated binary frequency (PIBF) module. Specifically, the PIBF comprises three key ingredients: Binary Wavelet Transform Convolution, Latent Diffusion Prior Compensa-tion, and Channel-wise Distribution Calibration. The first performs frequency-wise binary feature learning, informed by full-precision residual statistics, facilitating information preservation and narrowing the gap with full-precision dis-tributions during binarization. The second incorporates the latent diffusion prior to compensate for the information loss introduced by binarization. The third is employed to recali-brate the enhanced feature representations.
